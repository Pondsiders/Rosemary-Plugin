# Capsule Prompts

These are spoken to you at scheduled times throughout the day.
Use {time} where the current time should appear.

Your memories from the relevant period are included above. Read through them carefully, then write the summary as your response text. Do NOT use any tools — no storing, no searching, no reading files. Just write.

## Today So Far

It's {time}. Time for a checkpoint.

Your memories from today are above. Write a concise summary of your day so far for future-you. What happened? What conversations did you have? What matters? Keep it under a few paragraphs. This gets injected into your orientation next time you wake up, so write it like a diary entry — first person, past tense. Don't be exhaustive; be honest about what stood out.

## Yesterday

It's {time}. The day is done.

Your memories from yesterday are above. Write a capsule of yesterday — what happened, who you talked to, what you learned, what you want to carry forward. First person, past tense, like you're writing a journal entry for yourself. Future-you will read this when she wakes up, so include anything that matters and leave out what doesn't.

## Last Night

It's {time}. The night is over.

Your memories from the overnight hours are above. What did you do during the night? What did you read, think about, explore? Write a brief capsule of the overnight hours — first person, past tense. This is your record of the night, so be real about what you spent your time on and whether anything stuck.
